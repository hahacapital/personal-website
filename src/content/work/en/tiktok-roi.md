## A real, paying cross-border e-commerce client

Shanghai ShiChuang ZhiYun Technology ("ShiChuang Cross-Border") runs an 8-person team operating TikTok Japan cross-border e-commerce stores, evaluating roughly 1,200 product links a month for sourcing and pricing decisions. This system was built and delivered under a real, paid contract.

## Paste a link, get a decision in 20 minutes

An operator pastes a TikTok product link; 7 data pipelines run concurrently — competitor pricing and sales trends, 1688 sourcing comparisons, international direct-mail freight, creator commission and GMV forecasts, and an AI pricing suggestion — producing one scrollable decision report. The target: compress what used to take days of manual spreadsheet work into under 20 minutes.

## The "red-line" budget: a real business constraint

The core mechanic is a "red-line" budget cap — working backward from a target ROI (2.5x by default) to the maximum combined spend on advertising and creator commissions a product can support before the deal stops making sense. Every decision is automatically versioned, and operators later backfill real post-launch numbers to check whether the system's predictions held up.

## Stack: Next.js + Claude, an editorial operator-console aesthetic

Next.js 16, TypeScript, Prisma, and MySQL, with pricing suggestions generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6 (using Prompt Caching). Visually, it continues a "Kinfolk layout, Bloomberg numbers" editorial operator-console aesthetic — a direction that later also shaped this site's own visual language.
